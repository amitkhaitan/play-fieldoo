PLAY GUIDELINES FOR PLAYERS - HOW TO CONTACT AN AGENT

The key is to get contacted by an agent, scout or club and not to contact an agent! Getting contacted or finding the right contact is not a simple task, as you must take a lot of factors in account. It is hard work and it takes extra effort off pitch, but it is essential part of your career. Here are some guidelines to find the best possible agent or industry contact.

a.

Your Play profile is your football CV. When an agent or a scout ask you for your CV you send them your Play screen name link www.play.com/yourscreenname.

b.

Complete your football CV. Agent won’t even look at empty or half completed profiles. You should always try to fully complete all aspects of your Play profile. Don’t expect to get contacted if you don’t have a profile photo or you only entered two years of football in your career history.

c.

The most import part of your football CV is your promotional video. If you don’t have one create one as immediately.

‍d.

Always state your right career information. Every good agent will double check your career data and all information on you. Don’t lie or invent stuff.

‍e.

Know what you are looking for. If you are an upcoming player with big football dreams start by following and contacting an agent or agents from your region of activity. If you are seeking your last professional contract you should also try contacting agents from football exotic countries.

‍f.

All agents, scouts and clubs assess players the same way.

g.

Use Play market classifieds and apply for posts. Apply only to classifieds you fit for. Don’t apply to post searching for EU players if you are from Nigeria or Argentina. Just don’t do it!

h.

Never pay any money upfront to unknown agents, scouts or persons asking for it. Be careful, as not everybody has good intentions.

i.

When contacting an agent stick to the facts, be realistic and don't make abnormal demands. If you are currently playing in the 4th English Premier League.

j.

Look around. There are thousands of agent, scouts, and club representatives looking for football players on Play. Ask your Play network – friends and teammates for recommendation.

k.

Always do your own diligence. Play does verify agent information, but cannot and doesn’t guarantee their intentions.

l.

Indemnify Play. You must agree not to hold us responsible for anything. We are here to help, but all the decisions are you make on your own.

PLAY GUIDELINES FOR PLAYERS - HOW TO MAKE A PROMO VIDEO

The football player's promo video is one of the most important or even decisive things that make first impression on agents and scouts. That is why a player should have a quality promo video based on the qualities which agents and scouts like to see. Here are some guidelines on how to make the the best possible promo video to show your talent to the world. Promotional video is the most important part of your football CV. It is a short clip, made from various games (matches), where a player presents himself and emphasize his attributes.

a.

The promo video must point out the best attributes according to the player's position on the field, not just the best moves.

Learn on examples for: DEFENSIVE MIDFIELDER: defensive play, tackling, positioning, reading the game, changing the sides of the play, short passes, long passes, long range shooting, set pieces, etc.;

GOALKEEPER: command of area, best saves, one on one situations, reaction to high balls, good technic, long balls shooting, long ball throwing, etc.;

STRIKER: goals from all positions, with both legs, with head, defensive working habits, ability to keep the ball, one on one situation, set pieces, etc.

b.

The promo video has to be short. Ideally it should be from three (3) to maximum ten (10) minutes long.

c.

If possible, the promo video should contain the footage from the competitive matches only. Agents and scouts need to see player's reactions in real situations when there is lack of time and space and also reactions under pressure.

d.

NEVER MAKE the promo video that contains juggling, free styling, free kicks taking only, futsal playing or playing for fun with friends.

e.

A promo video must contain player's basic info. Add an ID to your presentation, stating who you are: your name and surname, date of birth, position, club (former clubs), nationality, height/weight and any other relevant information that might interest your viewer.

f.

Make sure that it is clearly visible who you are on the video. Post-production custom effects (for instance: highlighting your pitch position by a circle or a pointer,..) take some time, but are well worth it.

g.

The footage has to be the highest quality possible.

h.

Promo video should not be older than 1 year, and should be regularly updated. If possible, update promo video all the time (at least once a year, that is).

i.

Use only material that is relevant and up-to-date. Avoid using old footage; not only because a skilled agent or scout will know that the material is old, but also because – if everything is normal – newer footage should show better and new skills.

j.

Make sure you have enough material (your football matches) to make a good compilation out of it. If your games are not on TV and nobody is filming them, it is up to you to get somebody who will catch you on tape. In today’s digitalized world, it shouldn’t be a problem.

k.

Get to recordings of your matches or make sure (together with your team-mates) your matches are getting recorded.

ASK YOUR COACH. If you’re using videos to analyse your performance after the game then you can surely get a copy directly from your club.

GET IT DIRECTLY FROM A BROADCAST. If your matches are broadcasted live on TV, you can ask the TV station to give you a copy or have someone record the broadcast.

HIRE A CAMERAMAN or ask a friend. If above options aren’t possible than you can hire a cameraman. Talk to your teammates so you’ll be able to share expenses and get copies for everyone. If all of you pitch in you could even get a camera crew and end up with a professional recordings with multiple angles.

l.

Whatever problem you encounter, there is always a solution:

Don't have the footage from a competitive match? Use the footage from the friendly match.
Don't have a high quality camera? Use a smart phone.
Don't know how to cut the video? Use the YouTube Video Editor.

m.

When in doubt, contact us for advice. We are here to help, but all the decisions are you make on your own.

PLAY GUIDELINES FOR AGENTS

The key is to gain experience and further your career. Play adds another dimension to your hard work as an agent, it gives you an online presence and opportunity to find new players and expand your network. Getting hired or making partnerships is built on trust and transparency so make sure you have this mind when working of Play. Here are a few guidelines to help you make your Play experience better for everyone.

‍a.

Your Play profile is your business CV. When a club representative, a colleague or even a player ask you for your CV you send them your Play screen name link www.play.com/yourscreenname.

b.

Complete your profile. It's impossible to trust people you know nothing about so make sure players and other agents have your information on display.

Don’t expect to get contacted if you don’t have a profile photo or haven't written anything about yourself.

c.

Always state your right career information. Every player, club representative and fellow agent will double check your career data and information on you before considering doing business with you. So will us. Don’t lie or invent stuff.

d.

Know what you are looking for and what you offer. Fill in regions of activity you are or will be active in and be specific in your market posts.

e.

Use Play Market and post offers and apply for posts. Apply only to classifieds you fit for. Don’t apply if a post is looking for a partnership in Brazil and you work in Sweden.

f.

When contacting players and other agents stick to the facts, be realistic and don't make abnormal demands.

g.

Look around. There are tens of thousands of players looking for new opportunities and thousands of others working in football on Play. Ask your Play network – friends and colleagues for recommendation.

h.

Always do your own diligence. Play checks players' info and verifies agent information, but cannot and doesn’t guarantee their intentions.

i.
